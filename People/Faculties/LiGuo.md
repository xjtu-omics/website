                    <p>郭立，西安交通大学副教授，电信学院和生命学院硕士生导师。2006年本科毕业于西北农林科技大学植物保护学院，2012年毕业于美国宾夕法尼亚州立大学，获得植物病理学博士。2012年-2016年在美国马萨诸塞州大学从事植物病原基因组学的博士后研究。目前主要研究方向有：植物、真菌基因组学，次生代谢调控机制，寄主病原互相作用分子机制。研究成果已在Science, Nature Communications, New Phytologist, IEEE-Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, Methods in Molecular Biology, Frontiers in Plant Science, Molecular Plant Pathology等多个国际主流期刊发表学术论文15篇。目前主持国家自然科学基金青年项目一项，参与科技部重点研发专项一项。</p>

                <div id = "researches">
                    <h5>Research Focus</h5>
                    We use state-of-the-art omics approaches to investigate the fundamental biological questions. My long-term research interests focus on understanding the intricate system control of host immunity and pathogen virulence, especially plants and their microbial pathogens combining integrated omics data analysis, computational modeling and experimental validations, in order to help improve plant productivity and contain losses caused by abiotic and biotic stresses.  Current research projects include: <br/>
                   <img src ="images/Guo Li_research_1.jpg" alt = " " height="200" align="left"/><b>Comparative and functional genomics of medicinal plant opium poppy (<i>Papaver somniferum</i>)</b> to understand the genome evolution and transcriptional regulation for alkaloid biosynthesis in poppy plants. Recently, our lab has sequenced the first opium poppy genome (Guo et al. Science 362:6412) and revealed the complex evolutionary history of poppy genome and morphinan biosynthesis. Based on this reference genome, we are applying cutting-edge sequencing and genome-editing technologies to study the dynamics of genome evolution and regulatory mechanisms in this important medicinal plant, through collaboration with international and domestic colleagues from multiple disciplines.<br/>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <img src ="images/Guo Li_research_2.jpg" alt = " " height="200" align="right"/><b>Gene regulatory networks reconstruction in plants and pathogens:</b> Gene regulatory networks (GRNs) are in the center of every cell activity across wide range of organisms. Inferring and analyzing GRNs are not only fascinating scientific questions but also essential for improved and precise solution for medical and agricultural applications. We develop and implement algorithms to reconstruct GRNs in plants and pathogenic fungi from diverse experimental data such as transcriptome profiling, ChIP-seq datasets and protein-protein interaction data. This will help elucidate the systems control of important biological processes in medically and agriculturally important plants and microbes.<br/>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <br/>
                   <img src ="images/Guo Li_research_3.png" alt = " " height="200" align="left"/><b>Host-Pathogen Interactions using model plant Arabidopsis thaliana and filamentous fungus <i>Fusarium oxysporum</i></b>: Not all plants can be infected by all pathogens. The molecular mechanisms for host immunity against pathogenic and nonpathogenic fungi are not clear, but important for making effective disease control. We apply integrative approaches ranging from functional genomics to molecular genetics to dissect the molecular components in plants involved in sensing and responding to fungal infections.
